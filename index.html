Introduction

I am a Ph.D. student in Department of Computer Science, Fudan University since Fall 2012. My advisor is Prof. Min Yang, leader of System Software and Security Lab, and Chief Scientist of "973" project granted by Ministry of Science and Technology. Part of my work is also supervised by Prof. Guofei Gu from Texas A&M University, and Prof. Xiaofeng Wang from Indiana University at Bloomington. Before I came to Fudan, I got my B.E. degree in School of Information, Hainan University of China.

My research focuses on data-driven mobile security and privacy. I use program analysis techniques, machine learning, and natural language processing to solve practical security/privacy problems on mobile platforms. The majority of my on-going research focuses on identifying and protecting various emerging types of sensitive user data in mobile apps. Meanwhile, I conduct large-scale measurement studies over such private data, and assess the potential security threats of privacy leakage. The topics I am currently working on include

  • Privacy leakage detection on smart devices
  • Efficient program analysis for security enhancement
  • Software security compliance check
